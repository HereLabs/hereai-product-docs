This Privacy Policy explains how HereAI collects, uses, stores, shares, and deletes personal data in connection with Mira.

Mira is a workspace product used by businesses to create, review, approve, send, and measure go-to-market communications. If you use Mira through an organization, that organization may also control how your data is used in Mira.

## Scope

This policy applies to Mira and related HereAI websites, documentation, integrations, and support channels. It does not apply to third-party services that customers connect to Mira, such as Meta, Instagram, CRM systems, email service providers, or identity providers. Those services have their own privacy terms.

## Data we collect

### Account and workspace data

We collect data needed to create, secure, and operate Mira accounts and workspaces, including:

- Name, email address, company, role, and login information
- Workspace membership, permissions, and preferences
- Authentication, session, and security metadata
- Support requests and related communications

### Customer-provided business data

Customers may upload or connect business data so Mira can generate and route approved go-to-market materials. This can include:

- Product catalog records, positioning, pricing notes, and product metadata
- Customer, prospect, audience-member, or lead records
- CRM records and routing metadata
- Uploaded documents, brand assets, snippets, playbooks, and approval instructions
- Drafts, approvals, generated content, and audit history

### Instagram-connected data

When a customer connects an Instagram professional account, Mira may receive and store data authorized through Meta APIs or delivered by Meta webhooks, including:

- Instagram business account ID, username, and connected Page/account metadata
- OAuth access tokens and token-expiration metadata
- Instagram direct message sender identifiers, message IDs, timestamps, message text, attachments metadata, and delivery metadata
- Generated draft replies, approved replies, send receipts, and operational logs
- Review, approval, retry, and audit records connected to Instagram conversations

Mira uses Instagram-connected data only to provide the connected messaging workflow, including receiving DMs, drafting replies, enabling human review, sending approved replies, troubleshooting delivery, enforcing limits, and maintaining audit records. HereAI does not sell Instagram message data or Meta Platform Data.

### Usage, device, and security data

We collect operational data to keep Mira reliable and secure, including:

- IP address, browser, device, approximate location, and request metadata
- Product usage events, feature interactions, and performance telemetry
- Error logs, security logs, and abuse-prevention signals
- Cookie and session identifiers needed for authentication and product operation

## How we use data

We use personal data and customer data to:

- Provide, secure, monitor, and improve Mira
- Authenticate users and enforce workspace permissions
- Generate, review, approve, send, and measure customer communications
- Connect and operate integrations selected by customers
- Respond to support requests and service communications
- Detect, prevent, and investigate abuse, unauthorized access, fraud, and service misuse
- Meet legal, contractual, accounting, security, and compliance obligations

## AI processing

Mira uses AI systems to draft content, summarize context, classify records, and assist with operational workflows. Customer administrators control what data is provided to Mira and which workflows are enabled.

AI-generated output should be reviewed before external use. Mira is designed around human review and approval for customer-facing communications unless a customer explicitly enables an automated workflow within configured guardrails.

## Sharing and subprocessors

We share data only as needed to provide and protect Mira, including with:

- Cloud hosting, database, storage, monitoring, and security providers
- AI model providers used to generate or classify content
- Integration providers selected by customers, such as Meta, Instagram, CRM systems, identity providers, or email service providers
- Professional advisers, auditors, or authorities where legally required
- A successor entity in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets

See [Subprocessors](/mira/security/subprocessors/) for the current list of major third-party processors used in the Mira data path.

## Retention

We retain data for as long as needed to provide Mira, meet contractual obligations, maintain auditability, protect the service, and comply with law. Default retention practices are described in [Data handling & retention](/mira/security/data-handling-and-retention/).

Retention periods may vary by customer contract, workspace configuration, legal requirement, and data type.

## Deletion

Users can request deletion of personal data or Instagram-connected data by following the [Data deletion instructions](/mira/legal/data-deletion/).

After verification, we delete or anonymize personal data where we are able and legally permitted to do so. Some records may be retained for security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, accounting, audit integrity, dispute resolution, or backup recovery until the normal retention period expires.

## Security

Mira uses technical and organizational controls to protect customer data, including encryption in transit and at rest, tenant isolation, role-based access controls, audit logging, and secret storage for credentials. See [Security & trust](/mira/security/) for more detail.

No service can guarantee absolute security. If you believe data in Mira may have been accessed without authorization, contact **security@hereai.com**.

## International transfers

Mira may process data in the United States and other regions where HereAI or its subprocessors operate. Enterprise customers may request region-specific deployment options where available. See [GDPR](/mira/security/gdpr/) for information about EU data protection support.

## Your choices and rights

Depending on your location and relationship to Mira, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, export, restrict, or object to processing of your personal data.

If you use Mira through an organization, we may direct your request to that organization because it may be the controller of the workspace data. If you contact us directly, we will help route or process the request as appropriate.

## Children's data

Mira is intended for business use and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will update the "Last updated" date above and may provide additional notice when changes are material.
